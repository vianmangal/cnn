my understanding so far:

1) input image has a lot of pixels, to recognize the pattern easily instead of looking at each pixel we group them.
2) this group of pixels is passed through a filter (convolution layer) and a grid/matrix/stack-like structure is made for writing the 0s and 1s.
3) relu function is passed so only positive integers remain in the matrix. pooling is applied (majorly 2 types - avg/mean or max).
4) another filter is passed to "flatten" it as much as possible.
5) the final output is passed through a classic cnn with its weights and bias and the calculation shows the output in the final format of 0 or 1.
6) softmax for converting to probabilities of the image is shifted by certain pixels etc

droupouts- reduces neurons-  so the model does not memorize training images
